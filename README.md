VäXR was developed as an immersive geometry laboratory designed to support the teaching and learning of mathematics through embodied interaction and collaboration. The application is grounded in mathematical didactic research, with a particular focus on helping students develop conceptual understanding by actively constructing, manipulating, and discussing geometric forms rather than passively observing them. Instead of presenting geometry as static diagrams on a flat surface, VäXR places students inside a shared spatial, multi-user environment where shapes can be built, compared, sorted, and explored together.

The primary users of the application are students working in small groups and teachers facilitating structured lessons. Students engage directly with geometric tools and exercises, often in pairs, while communicating and reasoning together. Teachers guide the process, introduce tasks, manage group work, and support reflection and discussion. The overall purpose of the project is to create a learning environment where spatial reasoning, collaboration, and dialogue become central components of geometry education.
